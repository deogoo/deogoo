Vehicle Metal Recycling Utilizes Auto Shredding Line

Published on March 14, 2025

Cars have a limited lifespan, and after they are scrapped, they can be dismantled to recover metal materials. Therefore, shredding is indispensable in the field of resource recovery, with car shredders gradually becoming core equipment for processing end-of-life vehicles. Faced with an increasing number of obsolete cars, how to efficiently and environmentally dismantle these vehicles and recycle their materials has become one of the key challenges in the industry. With its superior performance, the car shredder not only quickly tears various metal structures, including car shells, but also effectively processes other types of scrap metals like sheet metal, oil drums, copper plates, aluminum blocks, and aluminum alloy doors and windows.

 


The dual-shaft metal shredder comes in multiple models to meet different recycling needs for scrap metals, with capacities ranging widely. The smallest model, GDI8, can process 5-10 tons of scrap metal per hour, while the largest model, GDI15, can efficiently handle 30-50 tons per hour. This means there's suitable equipment available whether it's for small-scale processing of sheet metal and oil drums or large-scale operations involving car shells and other heavy-duty metal waste.
Typically, users prefer mid-range models such as GDI10 or GDI12 that better match their production needs in terms of capacity. These models, capable of handling 10-20 tons/hour and 15-30 tons/hour respectively, meet the daily requirements of most users. Moreover, their moderate sizes make them easy to install and operate without occupying too much space. Consequently, machines with such intermediate processing capabilities are often more favored in actual procurement. However, the specific choice depends on individual processing volumes, budgets, and site conditions.
This dual-shaft metal shredder ensures efficient shredding of scrap metals of various shapes and sizes through its powerful shearing force and unique blade design. It not only significantly improves work efficiency and reduces storage space requirements but also greatly promotes the recycling of scrap metals, reducing environmental pollution. Additionally, the equipment is simple to operate and has low maintenance costs, making it an ideal choice that is essential in the modern metal recycling industry. Whether for small recycling stations or large processing centers, car shredders provide strong support for achieving a green economy.
